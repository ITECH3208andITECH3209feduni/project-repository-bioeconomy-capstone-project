BioEconomy Innovation Goals

Our aim is to utilize 75% of organic resources by 2025 to promote sustainability in Gippsland.

About Us

Empowering Gippsland's Sustainable Future Through Bioenergy

The BioEconomy Innovation Network is a collaboration between government, industry, academia, and the community. Aimed at activating sustainable, innovative, and growth through the BioEconomy sector in Gippsland. This initiative focuses on optimizing the highest value best of organic materials to create a sustainable circular economy that benefits both the environment and the community.

Our goals include:

  • Utilizing bioconversion technologies to upcycle organic resources into their highest-value products. This means ensuring that leftover agricultural products, such as broccoli pieces, are processed into high-value products like protein powders rather than used as low-value animal feed.
  • Collaborating with government, industry, academia, and community stakeholders to create sustainable solutions. By working together, we aim to develop cutting-edge solutions that address regional challenges and capitalize on local opportunities.
  • Enhancing the region's economic value by developing new industries and job opportunities through the bioeconomy sector. Our approach emphasizes the creation of a circular economy that supports environmental sustainability and community well-being.

Our Mission

Transforming Organic Materials for a Sustainable Future

Our mission is to transform organic resources into highest value products, reducing environmental impact and fostering economic growth. We aim to engage with local communities, industries, and governments to achieve a zero-resource-wastage goal by 2025.

According to the Gippsland BioHub Project Scoping Study, implementing bioconversion technologies can significantly reduce environmental impact and enhance regional economic growth. The study highlights the importance of integrating resource management strategies across various sectors to achieve sustainable outcomes.
