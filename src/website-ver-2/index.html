Gippsland's Organic Resource Breakdown

Insights into Resource Distribution

This chart illustrates Gippsland's organic materials, totaling 1,413,245 tonnes, from sectors excluding forestry. Key contributors include Dairy Processing (37%), Dairy Farming (21%), Waste Collection (16%), Manufacturing (14%), Poultry (10%), and Fruit & Veg Growing (2%).

Understanding this distribution helps identify which sectors are most involved in generating organic resources, guiding efforts to enhance sustainability and resource efficiency within the region.

Resource Volumes by Location Excluding Forestry

Insights into Resource Distribution

This data showcases the distribution of 1,451,245 tonnes of organic resources across Gippsland, excluding forestry. Key areas include:

  • Latrobe (265,840 T) - Dairy and Poultry
  • Baw Baw (481,377 T) - Dairy Processing
  • Wellington (219,467 T) - Mixed Agriculture
  • South Gippsland (463,782 T) - Dairy and Meat
  • East Gippsland (8,597 T) - Fruit & Vegetable Growing
  • Bass Coast (12,182 T) - Resource Collection

These insights allow for targeted resource management strategies, helping to optimize the use of organic materials and improve sustainability practices within each area.

Resource Volumes by Location Including Forestry

Expanded Insights into Resource Management

Including forestry, Gippsland's total organic resource volume significantly increases. Key areas include:

  • East Gippsland (974,597 T) - Forestry Residue
  • South Gippsland (762,800 T) - Mixed Agriculture and Forestry
  • Latrobe (269,173 T) - Dairy and Forestry
  • Baw Baw (481,377 T) - Dairy Processing
  • Wellington (467,115 T) - Mixed Agriculture
  • Bass Coast (12,182 T) - Resource Collection

These figures highlight the importance of forestry in the region's resource landscape, providing valuable data for sustainable resource planning and policy development.
